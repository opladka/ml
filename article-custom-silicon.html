Why GPUs Aren't Enough: The Case for Custom AI Silicon

MO
Marcus Osei
May 10, 2026
16 min read
Research

GPUs revolutionized AI because they could process thousands of independent mathematical threads in parallel. However, at their core, GPUs remain general-purpose vector processors. They require loading instructions, decoding vectors, and updating registers for every single operation.

Modern deep learning workloads are dominated by a single operation: **Matrix Multiplication**. Custom AI silicon, such as Google's Tensor Processing Unit (TPU) and specialized chips from Groq and Cerebras, bypass instruction fetch overhead by using hardware-wired math engines.

The Systolic Array Architecture

Unlike standard vector units that write outputs back to registers at each step, a **Systolic Array** passes data directly through a grid of Arithmetic Logic Units (ALUs) without storing intermediate results.

Data flows through the array like blood pulsing through a vascular system. Input matrices stream from left to right, while weight matrices stream from top to bottom. The results accumulate at each grid node and flow out of the boundaries, saving register bandwidth and power.

Why Matrix Engines Win

By hardwiring ALUs in a grid, systolic arrays eliminate the energy overhead of repeatedly reading from registers and instruction decoders. A single instruction launches a matrix multiplication that executes over thousands of cycles, achieving orders of magnitude improvement in throughput per watt.
